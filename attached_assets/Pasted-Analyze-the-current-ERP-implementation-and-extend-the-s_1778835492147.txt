Analyze the current ERP implementation and extend the system to support the following ownership, transfer, valuation, stock entitlement, payment, and dispute-handling business rules while preserving all existing working functionality.

IMPORTANT:

Reuse existing structures wherever possible. Avoid duplicate systems/modules/tables. Avoid unnecessary rewrites. Preserve existing workflows that already work correctly. Preserve existing OTP verification and approval workflow. Preserve existing valuation engine unless enhancement is necessary. Preserve existing percentage-based ownership architecture. Preserve existing UI structure as much as possible. Focus on extending business behavior safely and consistently. 

The goal is:

stable long-term ownership accounting, practical plantation operations, auditability, dispute resistance, accurate profit distribution, simple real-world usability. =================================== OWNERSHIP & TRANSFER BUSINESS RULES Ownership remains percentage-based. 

Users should primarily interact with:

ownership percentage, transfer percentage, profit percentage. 

Internally, system should maintain high precision ownership calculations to avoid rounding drift and long-term inconsistencies.

===================================

Ownership percentages must support high precision decimals. 

UI may show rounded values, but backend calculations should preserve precise values.

Purpose:

inheritance splitting, repeated transfers, long-term accounting consistency. 

===================================

Ownership changes must create immutable historical records. 

This includes:

transfers, inheritance, nominee succession, dispute settlements, corrections, dilution, ownership locks. 

Historical records must remain auditable and non-editable after finalization.

===================================

Ownership transfer must support BOTH: 

(A) Transfer by Percentage AND (B) Transfer by Transfer Value

Example:

transfer 10% OR transfer value ₹100000 

System should dynamically calculate equivalent values using the project valuation logic.

===================================

Transfer Value and Payable Amount are different concepts. 

TRANSFER VALUE:

ownership valuation basis, used for ownership percentage calculation. 

PAYABLE AMOUNT:

negotiated settlement/payment amount. 

PAID AMOUNT:

actual amount paid. 

Ownership percentage must always depend on: TRANSFER VALUE

NOT:

payable amount, paid amount, installment amount. 

===================================

Once ownership transfer is finalized through the existing OTP approval workflow: ownership transfer becomes fully effective immediately, ownership rights fully transfer, ownership percentage becomes active immediately. 

Whatever amount is actually paid shall be treated as accepted/final for ownership transfer purposes.

Future payment disputes must NOT automatically:

reverse ownership, recalculate ownership, delete transfer history. 

===================================

System must support transfer effective dates. 

Example: Transfer approved: 15 June

Effective from: 1 July

Rules:

old owner receives economic rights before 1 July, new owner receives rights from 1 July onward. 

This applies to:

rubber production, stock entitlement, profit distribution, sale proceeds. 

===================================

Rubber stock entitlement must support production-period ownership attribution. 

Stored rubber produced before transfer effective date may still economically belong to old owner.

System must support configurable handling of pre-transfer stored stock entitlement.

===================================

During transfer, system must allow: 

(A) Retain pre-transfer stored stock entitlement with previous owner

OR

(B) Transfer pre-transfer stored stock entitlement to new owner.

Example: 50 kg stored before transfer date.

Option A: old owner retains entitlement.

Option B: new owner receives entitlement.

System should track this clearly and auditable.

===================================

Optional advanced support: partial split of old stock entitlement. 

Example: 25 kg retained, 25 kg transferred.

Keep optional and lightweight.

===================================

Physical store location does NOT determine economic ownership. 

Economic entitlement depends on:

production timing, effective ownership date, transfer agreement, entitlement selection. 

===================================

System must track: production date, store entry date, unsold quantity, sold quantity, entitlement owner, transferred entitlement quantity, retained entitlement quantity, settlement history. 

===================================

Transfer validation must prevent: transfer beyond transferable ownership, transfer of locked ownership, transfer of disputed ownership. 

System should support:

transferable ownership, locked ownership, disputed ownership, reserved ownership. 

===================================

Pending transfers should temporarily reserve ownership to prevent double-transfer attempts. 

Example: same ownership portion should not be transferred multiple times simultaneously.

===================================

Profit distribution must use ownership percentages. 

System should support:

high precision calculations, effective dates, historical ownership periods, transfer timing, inheritance timing, stock entitlement separation. 

===================================

During ownership/payment disputes: 

DO NOT freeze entire project profit.

Only the disputed participant’s attributable share should be held.

Project operations must continue normally.

===================================

System should support: held profit, held sale proceeds, held distributions, for disputed ownership portions. 

Held amounts must remain:

traceable, auditable, releasable later. 

===================================

Historical ownership and transfer records must remain preserved during disputes. 

Disputes should NOT automatically:

reverse ownership, erase history, alter past snapshots. 

===================================

System should support: freezing further transfer of disputed ownership, temporarily holding disputed payouts. 

===================================

System should maintain a Held Distribution Ledger. 

Purpose: Track:

held profit, held sale proceeds, dispute-linked amounts, release decisions, settlement history. 

===================================

Transfer and ownership system should support: audit trails, timestamps, approval references, participant confirmations, settlement notes, payment references, proof attachments where applicable. 

===================================

System should preserve historical valuation snapshots used during transfers. 

Future valuation logic changes should not alter old transfer history.

===================================

Dashboard and transfer preview should clearly show relevant information such as: ownership percentage, transferable percentage, transfer value, payable amount, paid amount, outstanding amount, effective date, stock entitlement handling, held distributions where applicable. 

===================================

Existing OTP workflow must remain preserved. 

Current functionality already includes:

transferor OTP verification, receiving party review, receiving party rejection rights. 

Do NOT redesign this workflow. Only integrate new business rules around it.

===================================

Preserve simplicity. 

The ERP is intended for practical long-term plantation partnership operations.

Avoid:

overengineering, unnecessary complexity, blockchain/token systems, excessive workflow fragmentation. 

Focus on:

operational practicality, accounting stability, legal defensibility, user clarity, long-term maintainability.
