IMPLEMENT THE FINAL COMMERCIAL LOGIC ARCHITECTURE FOR BOTH PROJECT MODELS.

The ERP must support two fundamentally different commercial structures with strict behavioral separation.

==================================================
MODEL 1:
OWNERSHIP_CONTRIBUTION_MODEL
==================================================

--------------------------------------------------
CORE PRINCIPLE
--------------------------------------------------

This model creates actual proportional ownership participation based on validated contributions made BEFORE project maturity declaration.

Ownership share is calculated from total recognized contribution value.

--------------------------------------------------
VALID CONTRIBUTIONS
--------------------------------------------------

Recognized contribution types may include:

- Land Notional Value
- Cash contribution
- Labor contribution
- Material contribution
- Machinery contribution
- Service contribution
- Verified operational contribution
- Other approved contributions

--------------------------------------------------
LANDOWNER CONTRIBUTION
--------------------------------------------------

Landowner contribution includes:

1. Land Notional Value
PLUS
2. Any additional verified contribution

Examples:
- cash
- labor
- equipment
- operational support
- additional investment

Landowner ownership share must be calculated proportionally against total recognized contributions.

--------------------------------------------------
OWNERSHIP CALCULATION
--------------------------------------------------

Ownership percentage must be calculated using:

Participant Total Recognized Contribution
DIVIDED BY
Total Project Recognized Contributions

Only contributions made BEFORE maturity declaration are ownership-forming contributions.

--------------------------------------------------
MATURITY DECLARATION RULE
--------------------------------------------------

The system must support a formal:

PROJECT MATURITY DECLARATION

Once maturity is declared:

- ownership percentages become frozen/crystallized,
- contribution ownership formation stops,
- no new ownership contribution allowed,
- ownership recalculation stops.

--------------------------------------------------
POST-MATURITY PAYMENTS
--------------------------------------------------

Any payment, contribution, or financial support AFTER maturity declaration must NOT:

- create ownership,
- alter ownership percentage,
- alter crystallized shares.

Instead, post-maturity payments become:

PROJECT COST PAYMENTS

These must be:
- tracked separately,
- reimbursable by project,
- auditable.

--------------------------------------------------
POST-MATURITY REIMBURSEMENT RULE
--------------------------------------------------

Post-maturity payments must behave as:

recoverable project cost advances.

Examples:
- operational funding
- maintenance support
- emergency expense payments
- project expense settlement

The system must support:
- reimbursement tracking,
- payable balance,
- settlement history,
- reimbursement approval.

--------------------------------------------------
LCA RULES
--------------------------------------------------

LCA means:
LAND CONTRIBUTION ADJUSTMENT

Under this model:

- LCA is ENABLED
- LCA applies ONLY after maturity
- LCA is payable to eligible landowners
- LCA is paid BEFORE profit distribution

--------------------------------------------------
PROFIT DISTRIBUTION ORDER
--------------------------------------------------

Under OWNERSHIP_CONTRIBUTION_MODEL:

Distribution order:

1. Gross Revenue
2. Operational Cost Settlement
3. Reimbursable Cost Payment Settlement
4. LCA Payment
5. Remaining Net Profit
6. Profit Distribution by Ownership Percentage

--------------------------------------------------
IMPORTANT RULE
--------------------------------------------------

LCA must remain:
- separate from ownership percentage,
- separate from operational cost,
- separate from reimbursement.

==================================================
MODEL 2:
FIFTY_PERCENT_REVENUE_MODEL
==================================================

--------------------------------------------------
CORE PRINCIPLE
--------------------------------------------------

This model does NOT create ownership equity.

Instead:

- landowner receives fixed 50% gross revenue share
in exchange for land participation.

The remaining 50% forms an:

ECONOMIC PARTICIPANT POOL

--------------------------------------------------
LANDOWNER RIGHTS
--------------------------------------------------

Landowner receives:

1. Fixed 50% gross revenue share
for land participation / land notional value recognition.

2. May ALSO participate inside the economic participant pool.

Meaning:
A landowner may simultaneously:
- receive landowner 50% share,
AND
- hold contractual economic participation rights.

--------------------------------------------------
NO LCA RULE
--------------------------------------------------

Under this model:

LCA must NEVER apply.

Reason:
Landowner already receives:
- fixed gross revenue entitlement.

System must:
- hide LCA,
- disable LCA,
- block LCA calculations,
- prevent LCA schedules.

--------------------------------------------------
COST RESPONSIBILITY RULE
--------------------------------------------------

Under this model:

ALL operational costs are borne by:
LANDOWNER SIDE

This means:
- operational burden belongs to landowner responsibility,
- participant pool does not proportionally absorb operational burden unless separately configured.

--------------------------------------------------
REVENUE DISTRIBUTION ORDER
--------------------------------------------------

Under FIFTY_PERCENT_REVENUE_MODEL:

1. Gross Revenue
2. 50% Landowner Share
3. Remaining 50% Economic Participant Pool
4. Internal proportional distribution among economic participants

--------------------------------------------------
ECONOMIC PARTICIPANT POOL
--------------------------------------------------

Economic participant pool may contain:
- investors
- developers
- managers
- landowners
- strategic participants
- operational participants

Each participant may hold:
- contractual participation percentages.

These percentages:
- are distributable,
- transferable contractually,
- inheritable contractually,

BUT ARE NOT ownership equity.

--------------------------------------------------
STRICT NON-EQUITY RULE
--------------------------------------------------

Under this model:
the system must NEVER:
- create ownership percentage,
- crystallize ownership,
- calculate land equity,
- convert contribution into ownership.

==================================================
GLOBAL MATURITY RULE
==================================================

For BOTH models:

Only contributions made BEFORE maturity declaration may qualify as contribution participation.

After maturity declaration:

Any payment/support becomes:
- project cost payment,
NOT ownership-forming contribution.

==================================================
POST-MATURITY PAYMENT ENGINE
==================================================

Implement a separate engine for:

POST_MATURITY_PROJECT_COST_PAYMENTS

Features:
- reimbursement tracking
- payable balances
- reimbursement approvals
- audit logs
- settlement history
- linked expense records

These payments must NEVER:
- alter ownership,
- alter participant percentages,
- alter crystallized rights.

==================================================
STRICT SYSTEM SEPARATION
==================================================

The ERP must clearly separate:

1. Ownership Equity
2. Contractual Participation Rights
3. Operational Cost
4. Reimbursable Cost Advances
5. LCA
6. Profit Distribution
7. Revenue Sharing

These must NEVER silently mix together.

==================================================
AUDIT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Log:
- maturity declarations
- ownership crystallization
- post-maturity payments
- reimbursement settlements
- LCA calculations
- revenue distributions
- participant allocation changes
- contractual transfers
- cost settlements

with full permanent audit history.

==================================================
IMPORTANT SYSTEM RULE
==================================================

All downstream modules must inherit behavior from:
- project commercial model,
- lifecycle phase,
- maturity status,
- participant structure.

INCLUDING:
- accounting
- collection
- stock
- sales
- dashboard
- reporting
- reimbursement
- transfer systems
- settlement engine
- profit distribution
- buyer systems
- notifications
- permissions.
