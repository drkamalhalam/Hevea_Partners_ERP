PARTNER FINANCIAL LEDGER – ARCHITECTURE REFINEMENT WAVE (MANDATORY BEFORE IMPLEMENTATION)

This is a design-refinement task only. Do not begin implementation until every item below is incorporated into the architecture and reviewed.

IMPORTANT:

Do not remove any existing approved functionality. Do not change entitlement logic. Do not change ownership logic. Do not change inheritance logic. Do not change commercial-model rules. Do not introduce breaking API changes. Preserve append-only ledger architecture. Preserve reversal-based correction architecture. Preserve revenue attribution via entitlement consumption only. 

==================================================

REVENUE RECOGNITION TIMING CORRECTION ================================================== 

Current design states:

Revenue recognized on SaleCompleted.

Review existing Sale Rubber workflow and align financial recognition timing with approved sale lifecycle.

Revenue recognition must occur only when:

payment is confirmed/received AND sale reaches Approved/Paid financial state 

Revenue must NOT be recognized merely because a sale order exists.

Cancellation before financial recognition must create no ledger activity.

Document exact trigger state and update architecture accordingly.

================================================== 2. SEPARATE REVENUE, PROFIT AND DISTRIBUTABLE BALANCE 

Current design exposes:

Gross Revenue Distributable Balance 

This is insufficient.

Introduce clear financial hierarchy:

Gross Revenue minus Allocated Costs equals Net Profit

Net Profit minus Distribution Payments plus/minus Adjustments equals Distributable Balance

Update:

ledger documentation balance view dashboard calculations reporting definitions 

Required dashboard metrics:

Own Gross Revenue Own Allocated Costs Own Net Profit Own Distributed Amount Own Distributable Balance

================================================== 3. COST ALLOCATION AUDIT LAYER 

Before cost debits enter the partner ledger, create an immutable audit layer.

New table:

cost_allocation_lines

Purpose:

Store exact allocation basis used during allocation.

Suggested fields:

id project_id cost_reference_type cost_reference_id partner_id allocation_percentage allocated_amount allocation_date ownership_snapshot_reference created_at

Flow becomes:

Cost Event → Cost Allocation Lines → Ledger Entries

This table is the audit source for:

burden allocation expenditure allocation LCA allocation future cost allocation mechanisms 

No direct ledger insertion without allocation evidence.

================================================== 4. INTERNAL PARTNER PURCHASE REVENUE CATEGORY 

Current rule remains approved:

Seller receives revenue credit. Buyer receives entitlement. Buyer gets no ledger entry.

However reporting must distinguish:

External Sale Revenue Internal Transfer Revenue

Required:

Revenue category field or equivalent reporting classification.

Project financial reports must display:

External Revenue Internal Transfer Revenue Total Revenue

Project profit calculations must not accidentally treat internal transfers as external project income.

================================================== 5. HELD / BLOCKED DISTRIBUTABLE BALANCE SUPPORT 

Introduce financial-hold capability.

Supported hold reasons:

Dispute Inheritance dispute Court order Governance hold Administrative hold

Held amounts must:

remain visible remain auditable not disappear from history not alter revenue history 

Required outputs:

Total Balance Held Balance Available Distributable Balance

Design must support future hold workflows.

================================================== 6. PROJECT CLOSURE SNAPSHOT EXPANSION 

Current closure snapshot stores only distributable balance.

Expand closure snapshot.

For every partner store:

Sheet Entitlement Scrap Entitlement Gross Revenue Allocated Costs Net Profit Distributed Amount Remaining Distributable Balance

Closure snapshot must support future reconstruction even if operational records are archived.

================================================== 7. REIMBURSEMENT ACCOUNTING TRACEABILITY 

Current architecture routes reimbursement through generic adjustments.

Refine design.

Create explicit reimbursement allocation evidence.

Suggested flow:

Reimbursement Event → Reimbursement Allocation Lines → Ledger Entries

Store:

source reimbursement affected partners allocation basis allocated amount

Ledger entries must always trace back to reimbursement evidence.

Generic adjustment records must not become the primary reimbursement audit source.

================================================== 8. PROJECT PROFIT DEFINITION 

Formally define project profit.

Review treatment of:

external sales internal partner purchases reversals cancellations reimbursements 

Provide final formula.

Document:

Gross Revenue Project Costs Net Project Profit

Ensure internal partner purchases cannot artificially inflate project profit.
